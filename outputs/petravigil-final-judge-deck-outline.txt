PETRAVIGIL - FINAL HACKATHON JUDGE DECK

Audience: Hackathon jury
Length: 5 minutes
Communication job: Prove one safe, evidence-first decision path for a Hormuz disruption. The product is a local, analyst-confirmed prototype; it does not claim live intelligence feeds or autonomous procurement execution.

SLIDE 1 - PetraVigil: make resilience a rehearsed decision
Time: 0:00-0:20
Objective: State the decision problem and the honest product boundary.
Exact content: "When a disruption signal appears, procurement should not improvise." / "PetraVigil turns a source-labelled signal into a reviewable decision - or says not enough evidence yet."
Visual/layout: One dark, spare route-to-decision line. Add a visible badge: "Local prototype - no live feeds or procurement execution."

SLIDE 2 - The one case: a Hormuz disruption signal
Time: 0:20-0:45
Objective: Ground the demo in one coherent case, not a collection of dashboard widgets.
Exact content: User-entered disruption text, an offline source-labelled Hormuz replay, and a local Decision Clock. Explain that the replay is a rehearsal artefact, not AIS or current vessel tracking.
Visual/layout: One signal card flowing into one decision timeline.

SLIDE 3 - AI proposes structure; the analyst and constraints decide
Time: 0:45-1:20
Objective: Show bounded AI rather than AI theatre.
Exact content: Gemini extraction when configured; labelled deterministic fallback otherwise. Display extracted entities, confidence, unknowns, and the analyst confirmation gate before simulation.
Visual/layout: Left: raw user-entered signal. Centre: proposed structure. Right: analyst confirmation and deterministic checks.

SLIDE 4 - Reproducible scenario, visible assumptions
Time: 1:20-2:00
Objective: Establish technical credibility without claiming a market forecast.
Exact content: Analyst-confirmed closure, duration, alternative capacity, elasticity, simulation count, and seed feed a NumPy Monte Carlo scenario. Results are simulated; inputs and unknowns remain visible.
Visual/layout: Compact assumption rail -> scenario result -> confidence/unknowns rail.

SLIDE 5 - A recommendation must be physically feasible
Time: 2:00-2:35
Objective: Demonstrate the OR-Tools portfolio comparison.
Exact content: Seeded refinery-grade compatibility and route capacity create alternatives and rejected options. Supplier availability and prices are not live claims.
Visual/layout: Three minimal portfolio cards: selected, alternative, rejected with a concise constraint reason.

SLIDE 6 - The most important output can be a refusal
Time: 2:35-3:15
Objective: Make the no-recommendation safety gate memorable.
Exact content: A capacity-shortfall case returns `NO_RECOMMENDATION_YET`, the specific blockers, and next actions. It never fabricates a portfolio just to keep the dashboard green.
Visual/layout: A single red/neutral safety-gate panel, then an action checklist.

SLIDE 7 - Do not double-count a scarce route
Time: 3:15-3:50
Objective: Show the shared multi-refinery capacity drill.
Exact content: Jamnagar, Paradip, and Kochi compete for the same seeded route capacity. The local allocator reports served/unserved volume and cargo contention rather than allocating the same route independently to every refinery.
Visual/layout: One shared route pool branching to three refinery demand bars.

SLIDE 8 - Finite bridge, human decision
Time: 3:50-4:20
Objective: Make governance tangible.
Exact content: The single-refinery SPR contingency is finite, opt-in, and authorization-dependent. The final action is Approve, Defer, or Reject in a local decision record; no order is placed.
Visual/layout: A finite bridge meter ending in a human approval signature.

SLIDE 9 - Evidence of implementation, then a clear production path
Time: 4:20-4:40
Objective: Separate working proof from roadmap.
Exact content: "37 backend tests passed in the last verified local run." / FastAPI, NumPy, OR-Tools, SQLite, and optional Gemini are active. Live data adapters, calibration, RBAC, enterprise storage, and integrations are the next validated steps.
Visual/layout: Two columns: "Proven here" and "Validate next." No claims about latency, ROI, or inactive infrastructure.

SLIDE 10 - Close: accountable speed, not false autonomy
Time: 4:40-5:00
Objective: Land the reason to choose the project.
Exact content: "PetraVigil proves a safer decision workflow: label evidence, confirm assumptions, respect finite capacity, refuse unsupported answers, and keep a human accountable."
Visual/layout: One central statement and the six-stage flow beneath it.

JURY REVIEW
The narrative supports innovation through explicit safety behaviour, business relevance through a procurement decision workflow, technical excellence through deterministic constraints plus bounded AI, and UX through one understandable case. Do not claim live AIS/news/market data, production integrations, measured response time, ROI, or active multi-agent infrastructure.
